It's recommended to engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity per week, spread throughout the week for optimal health benefits.

Warming up increases blood flow to your muscles, loosens joints, and prepares your body for exercise, reducing the risk of injury.

Set clear fitness goals, find a workout buddy, vary your routine, and reward yourself for accomplishments to maintain motivation.

A combination of aerobic exercises (e.g., running, cycling) and strength training (e.g., weightlifting) is most effective for weight loss and overall health.

Proper form, gradual progression, listening to your body, and incorporating rest days are key to preventing injuries during workouts.

Nutrition plays a crucial role in supporting your fitness goals. Balanced meals with a mix of protein, carbs, and healthy fats help fuel your workouts and aid recovery.

Rest periods between sets can vary based on your goals. For muscle hypertrophy, 1-2 minutes of rest is common, while strength-focused workouts may require longer rest intervals.

Most people can meet their nutritional needs through a well-balanced diet. Supplements should only be considered when advised by a healthcare professional.
